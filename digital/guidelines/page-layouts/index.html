The new brand is focusing on white. Our new redesigned pages on bell.ca will be a lot more white than the previous generations of design. However some exceptions exists. They following examples would illustrate the various combination possible.

Article text & white space on pages

Text paragraphs width: There is a lot of research around best practices for readability on the web. To optimize readability its best to limit the width of the container text. By doing so, we provide a better reading experience for users. To my knowledge, scrolling on the page does not have a significant impact on the reading experience. The new bell brand takes this into account and limits characters to around 78 per line (so for paragraphs and articles this means a container of max 590px). This is something we cannot deviate from.

This interesting research article from a very good web usability research institute if you would like to read more about the subject https://baymard.com/blog/line-length-readability

White space

White space is actually a major component of design (in the web and everywhere else). To name a few benefits of using white space, it reduces cognitive noise on the screen and provides emphasis on the content we want the customer to see and read. It should be embraced rather than feared.

*Notice how both articles I've shared show a max container width that allows around 70-74 characters per line. It's really makes it easier to read.
