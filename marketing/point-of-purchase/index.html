To ensure we stand out and stay true to the Bell brand, messaging must be simple, clean and direct.

1

Bell logo

Use the Bell logo without tagline in a prominent sign-off position at the top or bottom of the layout.

The Bell logo is not required on posters displayed in Bell stores unless a co-branded poster or leaseline element or in windows.

Read more about Bell logo.

2

Bellement

A cropped portion of the Bell logo used in a relevant way can be used as a graphic device.

3

Photography

For screens, use bright and interesting royalty-free stock or content provided by Bell. Lifestyle photography is to be used on white backgrounds only.

4

Headline

It should be clear first, clever second and communicate a product benefit. Headlines can also incorporate prices/offers when necessary. Avoid retail clichs like deal of the century.

Minimum size (based on cap height) for the headline should be the height of the Bell logo.

5

Subhead

If required, a brief descriptive follow-up to the headline, usually communicating the product benefit.

6

Body copy

Communicate simply and with the right tone and manner. Only used in particular instances when more details are required.

7

Price point

Conveys the offer and/or price point for a product or service. Please follow Price point treatment guidelines on the following pages.

8

Call-to-action

Actionable information, typically Ask us for details. or Ask us how.
