Radio

Hmm. Bellements don’t exactly translate into radio. That’s okay. We’ve found another way to conjure up the Bell universe in radio.

  • Optimistic
  • Benefit focused
  • Solutions oriented

English

How can Bell make your day better? Have a listen and learn. Each radio spot begins with a friendly, confident announcer explaining “How Bell makes _________ better.” What’s made better could be product or service-related, a consumer insight, or something as simple as the season.

The body of the radio commercial articulates what’s being made better in relation to a Bell service or product – all in an engaging, entertaining way. The spot concludes with the announcer reminding listeners that “__________ just got better.” An example is provided on the right for your reference.

English example

Lead: How Bell makes Wireless better .

Anncr: Bell's network is now up to 2 times faster. So you can…

Fact check your friend 2 times faster when he says watermelons are berries.

And GPS a route to the cottage 2 times faster when your boyfriend gets you lost because he doesn’t need directions.

Or download a movie 2x faster before hopping on the subway for the long commute home. [Typing noises on a phone] Oh wow, watermelons actually are berries.

Lead: Canada’s best national network is now up to 2 times faster. Visit a Bell store or Bell.ca/network for details. Bell wireless just got better.

French

Always start with a 1.5-second musical intro, followed by a vignette wherein the actors, who consistently speak with a Québec accent, are credible and natural. We segue into the leitmotif.

This musical refrain will continue to the end of the ad, and serves as the melodious backdrop to the announcer, who delivers the offer and invariably concludes with, “La vie est Bell.”
